- Game:
-- Layout: base unit is squares, squares are YxY pixels
-- Time: moves forward 1 unit when Player moves or interacts with an object, 1 unit of time is a turn

- Character:
-- Player: 1 square in size
-- Player: moves 1 square per turn
-- Player: starts in main room
-- Player: can push moveable objects
-- Player: has a map covered in fog of war that is uncovered as rooms are discovered
-- Player: can hold X number of objects (TODO: determine number of inventory slots Player has)
-- Player: can use interactive objects
-- Enemy: 1 square in size
-- Enemy: moves 1 square per turn
-- Enemy: starts randomly within the house but at least 2 rooms from Player
-- Enemy: kills Player when adjacent causing game over
-- Enemy: can lock and unlock doors
-- Enemy: can take passageways


- House: location the game takes place, Player is trapped in house, goal for Player is to escape
-- Room: has X <= 4 doors
-- Room: can contain 0 <= X <= Y number of traps
--- Constant Rooms:
---- Hallway: connects 2 - 4 rooms, does not contain traps or any objects
---- Main Atrium: room where Player starts, contains 1 staircase and 2 doors, and the final door
---- Passageway: secret passage that leads to another room or floor
----- One way: Player can only travel through passageway in one direction
----- Two way: Player can travel back and forth through the passageway
--- Extra Large Room:
---- Greenhouse: can contain objects, can contain passageway
--- Large Room:
---- Master Bedroom: always connected to Master Bathroom, can contain objects, can contain passageway
---- Gallery: can contain passageway, can contain objects
---- Kitchen: can contain passageway
---- Den:
---- Dungeon:
--- Medium Room:
---- Master Bathroom: always connected to the master bedroom, can contain objects
---- Bedroom: dead-end room, can contain objects
---- Sunroom: dead-end room
---- Meat Locker:
---- Cellar:
--- Small Room:
---- Closet: can contain passageway
---- Bathroom: always connected to a hallway, can contain objects
-- Floor: house contains 3 floors
--- 2nd floor:
--- Main floor: can contain Small, Medium, Large, and Extra Large rooms
--- Basement: contains Dungeon, Meat Locker, and Cellar
 

- Objects: can be fixed or moveable, interactive or not interactive, block or not block vision
-- Key: can lock or unlock doors
-- Door: can be locked, unlocked, blocked, opened, or closed
-- Trap: can be triggered by the Player
--- Trap Triggers:
---- Pressure Plate: triggers trap when stepped on by the Player
---- Wall Switch: triggers trap when interacted with by the Player
---- 
--- Types of Traps:
---- Noise Trap: when triggered makes a loud noise giving the Enemy the exact position of the user and 2 extra turns 
---- Poison Gas Trap: when triggered begins filling room with gas, gas can escape through open doors
---- Trap Door Trap: drops the user down 1 level, cannot be placed in basement
-- Puzzle Object: object that helps complete part of the puzzle to beat the game
-- Staircase: allows the Player to ascend or descend a level in the house
